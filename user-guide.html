1 · Before you start

The Odour Intensity Recorder structures a five-minute observation into thirty samples taken every ten seconds. Each sample is your live rating of odour intensity from 0 to 6, and the app aggregates these into an average intensity, a percentage of time the odour is recognisable, and a final IAQM odour effect classification.

What you need

  • An iPhone (or any phone with a modern browser — the tool is designed for iOS Safari).
  • Roughly six minutes per location: thirty seconds to enter conditions, five minutes to record, and a minute to classify.
  • Optional but recommended: location permission for GPS coordinates.
  • A reasonable estimate of meteorological conditions on site. The app helps with wind strength and stability category — see those sections.

What it produces

A single CSV file per session containing every sample, the meteorological conditions, the IAQM exposure and effect classification, and the surveyor's identity. The filename defaults to Location 1 YYYY-MM-DD HH-MM.csv with the survey start time embedded for traceability.

2 · Installing on your iPhone

The app runs in the browser; there is no App Store download. For best reliability in the field, add it to your home screen so it launches as a full-screen application without browser chrome.

  1. Open the recorder URL in Safari (not Chrome — Safari handles Add to Home Screen better on iOS).
  2. Tap the Share button (square with up-arrow) at the bottom of the screen.
  3. Scroll down and tap Add to Home Screen.
  4. Confirm the name (Odour Recorder) and tap Add.
  5. Launch from your home screen. The app opens full-screen, looks like a native app, and continues to work offline once loaded.
Tip Once added, the app keeps your surveyor name in local storage so you don't re-enter it for each session. Different surveyors using the same device can simply edit the name on the setup screen.

3 · Setup screen

The first screen captures who is surveying, where they are, and the sensitivity classification of the receptors nearby. All three are mandatory.

  1. Surveyor Name Your name as it should appear on the survey record. Saved on the device, so subsequent sessions auto-fill this field.
  2. Location Name A short identifier for the survey point — e.g. Plant 3, North Boundary or Receptor R12. Used in the filename and the CSV summary.
  3. Receptor Sensitivity The IAQM classification of the receiving land use: High for residential, hospitals, schools and tourist or cultural sites; Medium for workplaces, commercial or retail areas, and recreation fields; Low for industrial sites, farms, footpaths and roads. This setting determines how the final odour effect is computed.
  4. Next Disabled until all three fields are filled. Mandatory fields show their state visually: red border for empty, green for completed.

4 · Conditions screen

This screen captures the meteorological conditions that will appear in the survey record. Six fields are mandatory; two (Humidity and Barometric Pressure) are optional.

  1. Wind strength reference Tap to expand the Beaufort scale with field-observable cues for each band (smoke vertical, leaves rustling, dust raised, branches in motion). Used to pick the correct Wind Strength below.
  2. Wind Direction Sixteen-point compass plus Variable and Calm. The direction the wind is coming from.
  3. Wind Strength Beaufort band 0–8+. Read the reference above if you are unsure how to estimate it.
  4. Cloud Cover Four buckets: Clear, Partly cloudy, Mostly cloudy, Overcast. Plain-language descriptions accompany each option.
  5. Rainfall None, Drizzle, Light rain, Moderate rain, Heavy rain.
  6. Suggest from sun + wind + cloud Computes the Pasquill-Gifford stability category from time of day, location, wind speed, and cloud cover. Requires Wind Strength and Cloud Cover to be filled — the button shows a warning if either is missing.
  7. Derivation rationale Plain-language summary of the inputs used — time of day with sun elevation, Beaufort band converted to m/s, cloud bucket, and the location source. Gives you everything you need to validate or override the suggestion.
  8. Stability Category Pasquill-Gifford A–F. Pre-filled by Suggest; you can edit manually if local conditions warrant it. Border turns green once filled.
  1. Temperature (°C) Mandatory. One decimal place is supported.
  2. Humidity (%) Optional. Whole numbers.
  3. Barometric Pressure (hPa) Optional. Useful for atmospheric record but not required for the IAQM calculation.
  4. On-site Operations & Activities Free text. Describe what is happening on site — slurry spreading, kitchen exhaust, processing line running, etc.
  5. Surrounding Area Activities Free text. Note other potential odour sources nearby — neighbouring farms, traffic, vegetation.
  6. Start Recording Validates all mandatory fields and requests GPS permission, then begins the five-minute observation. If a mandatory field is missing, an alert lists what is needed.

5 · Recording screen

The five-minute observation. The slider on the right controls the live odour intensity rating; samples are captured every ten seconds and the screen pulses an audible pip-pip-pip-BEEP on each capture.

  1. Skip Top-right. Ends the session early after a confirmation, filling remaining samples with the current intensity. Use sparingly.
  2. Master timer Counts down from 5:00. Turns red in the final 30 seconds.
  3. Sub-timer (10-second window) Counts down to the next sample. Turns red at 3, 2, and 1 seconds with a pip on each.
  4. Beep toggle Tap to turn the audible cue on or off. Helpful in residential surveys where loud beeps would draw attention.
  5. Progress bar Visual track of session progress.
  6. Intensity name and description The label and IAQM definition for the currently selected intensity.
  7. Live intensity number Mirror of your slider position, large and central for at-a-glance reading.
  8. Slider Drag up or down to set the live intensity. Range 0–6. The threshold at intensity 4 (the IAQM "recognisable odour" line) is marked with an amber tick.
  9. Anomaly flag Tap when something contaminates the observation — a tractor passes, an extractor cycles on, neighbouring activity changes. The screen flashes red and the count increases. Use as many times as needed.
  10. Sample counter Shows progress through the thirty samples. During the first ten seconds it reads "Get ready · First sample at 00:10".
Note The screen will not sleep during recording — the app holds a screen wake lock for the duration. You can lock the phone deliberately, but doing so will pause the timer and you may lose samples; iOS will throttle background tabs. Best practice: leave the phone unlocked and the app foreground for the full five minutes.

6 · Anomaly review

If you flagged any anomalies during recording, this screen appears next. Each flag is listed with its time and the intensity at the moment of the flag, and you can add a brief note explaining what happened.

  1. Flag entry One row per flag, showing the sequence number, the time within the session (e.g. 2:30), and the recorded intensity at that moment.
  2. Note input Optional. Brief description — tractor passing, extractor cycled on, door opened next door. Text is preserved verbatim in the CSV.
  3. Remove All Clears every flag if they were tapped in error. Asks for confirmation.
  4. Continue Proceeds to the hedonic tone classification. Notes are not required to continue.

7 · Hedonic tone classification

The hedonic tone is the surveyor's overall impression of pleasantness. One of three categories is mandatory; an optional checkbox captures whether the tone varied during the session.

  1. Pleasant Agreeable or appealing odour.
  2. Neutral Neither pleasant nor unpleasant.
  3. Unpleasant Disagreeable or offensive odour. The most common category in regulatory surveys.
  4. Tone varied during session Tick this if the character changed during the five minutes — for example, "started pleasant floral, became chemical after three minutes". Reveals a free-text description.
  5. Variation description Optional free text. Captured in the CSV as a separate field for QA review.

8 · Descriptor selection

The next screen offers a searchable list of 141 odour descriptors with their hedonic scores from the Dravnieks (1985) atlas. You can select one or many; the mean score appears in the results.

  1. Search box Filters the list as you type. Useful for descriptors you are confident about.
  2. Selected descriptor chip Each selection shows as a green-accented card with the hedonic score and a remove cross. Tap the cross or the row in the list to deselect.
  3. Descriptor list Sorted with the most negative (most unpleasant) descriptors first — Cadaverous, Putrid, Sewer odour, Cat urine, etc. — since most regulatory surveys are of unpleasant sources. Pleasant descriptors are at the bottom of the list.
  4. Additional / alternative description Free text for descriptors not in the list, or for nuance the standard list cannot capture.
  5. View Results Enabled when at least one descriptor or any free text is provided.
Tip For mixed odours, picking two or three descriptors gives a mean score that captures the blended character better than forcing a single best-fit.

9 · Results & CSV download

The results screen presents the IAQM outputs at the top, the conditions and descriptors below, and every sample in a scrollable table. The filename pre-fills with the location and the timestamp of when you tapped Start Recording.

  1. Average Intensity (Imean) Mean of the thirty samples, rounded to nearest integer 0–6, with the IAQM strength label.
  2. Odour Time (t≥4) Percentage of samples at intensity 4 or higher — i.e. with a recognisable odour. Shows the absolute count alongside.
  3. Odour Exposure IAQM exposure category derived from Imean and t≥4 using the IAQM exposure matrix.
  4. Odour Effect The final IAQM outcome — Negligible, Slight adverse, Moderate adverse, or Substantial adverse — derived from the exposure category and the receptor sensitivity. Colour-coded.
  5. Hedonic Tone & Descriptors Mirror of your earlier classifications, with the mean Dravnieks score where applicable.
  6. Sample table Every ten-second sample with its intensity and label. Scroll to review.
  7. File Name Pre-filled as Location 1 YYYY-MM-DD HH-MM. Edit if you prefer a different name; the .csv extension is added automatically.
  8. Download CSV On iOS, opens the share sheet so you can save to Files, email, AirDrop, or upload to your CDE. On desktop, downloads the file directly.

After downloading

Once you confirm the save, the app shows a confirmation screen with the saved filename and a New Session button to start the next location.

10 · What to do with the CSV

Each session produces a single CSV file with three logical sections: a per-sample table at the top, a Summary block, and a Meteorological Conditions block. If anomalies were flagged, an Anomalies block is appended.

Opening the file

Excel or Numbers open the CSV directly. Google Sheets works via File → Import. The file uses standard RFC 4180 quoting, so any field containing commas or quotes is wrapped properly and will not break the column structure.

What each section contains

Per-sample rows (top of file)

ColumnMeaning
TimeMm:ss within the session, e.g. 2:30
SecondsNumeric seconds elapsed, useful for plotting
Intensity0–6 integer, the surveyor's rating at that sample
Odour StrengthText label corresponding to the intensity
Anomalytrue if the sample window contained a flag, otherwise empty

Summary block

Key-value pairs: app version, schema version, date, time, surveyor, location, GPS coordinates and accuracy, receptor sensitivity, the four IAQM outputs, dominant hedonic tone, variation flag and description, descriptor list with individual and mean scores, and the additional free-text description.

Meteorological Conditions block

Wind direction, wind strength, cloud cover, atmospheric stability, rainfall, temperature, humidity, and pressure as captured on the Conditions screen.

Site Operations block

The two free-text fields describing on-site activities and the surrounding area.

Anomalies block (only if flagged)

One row per flag with sequence number, time, intensity, and the surveyor's note.

Working with multiple sessions

Each survey is one CSV. For studies covering multiple receptors or repeated visits, keep the files in a single folder named after the project and use the standardised filenames as your primary index. The App Version and Schema rows at the top of every Summary make it easy to filter by methodology version if you re-run the analysis later.

Schema versioning The current schema is version 2. If a future app version changes the column structure or methodology — for example, by adding new fields or revising calculations — the schema number will increment. Any downstream parser should branch on this value.

11 · Troubleshooting

The pip and beep don't play

iOS Safari requires audio to be unlocked by a user tap before any sound will play. The first tap on Start Recording does this automatically. If you toggled the beep off and back on during the session and the sound is still silent, your iPhone may be in silent mode — flip the side switch above the volume keys.

GPS permission was denied

The session continues without GPS. The CSV records empty coordinates. To re-enable: Settings → Privacy & Security → Location Services → Safari Websites → Allow.

The screen went to sleep during recording

The app holds a screen wake lock for the duration of the recording, so this should not happen. If it does — typically because iOS Low Power Mode overrides wake locks — disable Low Power Mode (Settings → Battery) before starting a session.

I tapped Skip by accident

Skip requires a confirmation tap before it ends the session, so a single accidental tap won't trigger it. If you confirmed and want the data back, the session has been completed with the current intensity filled into the remaining samples — the data is real for the elapsed time, but the unrecorded portion is approximate. For a critical survey, restart the session.

The CSV won't save / the share sheet didn't appear

On iOS, tap Download CSV; the share sheet appears with options to Save to Files, Mail, AirDrop, etc. If the sheet doesn't appear, check that you are running the app from your Home Screen icon (not a private Safari tab — private tabs block some share APIs).

I lost the session because the phone restarted

The current build does not persist in-progress sessions across reloads. Once Start Recording has been tapped, keep the app foreground until the CSV has been saved. If the phone restarts mid-session, the data is unrecoverable — restart the survey.

The Suggest button says "Need: …" and won't suggest a stability category

Suggest needs at least Wind Strength and Cloud Cover. Pick those two from their dropdowns and tap Suggest again.

I'm getting a red border around fields I haven't filled

That's the design — red borders mark mandatory fields that are still empty. They turn green once filled. The Start Recording button will list any missing fields if you try to start before completing them.
